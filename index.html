Our Story
✦

Some things are written before you’re old enough to read.

That photo above is me as a baby, held by my father — surrounded by tobacco shelves in our family’s store. I didn’t choose this industry. It chose me before I could walk.

My father spent decades running gas stations across northern New Jersey. In 2019, he and my uncle added a small humidor to one of the locations in Maywood — just to see. Within a year that one humidor turned into seventeen. The demand was real, and so was the passion.

That success led them to something bigger. They opened a wholesale and retail operation right here in Pine Brook — first under the name Cigar Outlet, with a retail showroom up front and a full wholesale operation in the back. I started working here from day one, learning the craft, learning the business, learning what makes a great cigar and what makes a great experience.

Then in September 2025, my father and uncle decided to move on. The store went up for sale. I watched offer after offer come in — lowball after lowball — and something in me said no. This place deserved better.

So I bought it. It wasn’t cheap. But I call it the price of freedom.

That’s how The Cigar Vault was born. A family story that started in a gas station, built through years of hard work, and now carried forward by someone who grew up in it — and who shows up every single day because this isn’t just a business. It’s personal.

My name is Selim, and The Cigar Vault is my life’s work. Come in and introduce yourself — I’ll be the one behind the counter.

Personal. Passionate. Premium.

Selim — Owner, The Cigar Vault

Help Fix NJ's Cigar Tax

✦

New Jersey currently taxes premium cigars at 30% of the wholesale price — one of the highest rates in the nation. This punishes independent retailers and drives loyal customers across state lines to shop elsewhere.

30% Current NJ Wholesale Tax on Cigars

NJ Senate Bill S2569 would cap the cigar tax at $0.50 per cigar — a fair, predictable rate that protects consumers, keeps local tobacconists in business, and brings New Jersey in line with states that respect the premium cigar community.

Eleven states across the country are currently pushing for this exact reform. Louisiana has already passed it. It's time New Jersey followed.
